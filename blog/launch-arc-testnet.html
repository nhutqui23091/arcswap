ArcSwap is officially live on Arc Testnet

ArcSwap is live on Arc Testnet. The first wave of public surfaces is open today — Unified Balance powered by Circle Gateway, a native Trade venue with CCTP V2 bridging, and a one-click ERC-20 launcher — with more to follow. Connect any wallet to start; ArcSwap is non-custodial and there is no account to create.

ArcSwap Team · May 10, 2026 · 4 min read

Today we're opening ArcSwap to everyone. ArcSwap is officially live on Arc Testnet — a non-custodial frontend that turns Arc into a single venue for stablecoin operations.

Connect any EIP-1193 wallet, request testnet USDC from Circle's faucet, and every public surface is immediately usable. There is no account to create and no protocol token.

TL;DR
ArcSwap is a non-custodial frontend that turns Arc into a single venue for stablecoin operations. Today's launch opens the first public surfaces — Balance, Trade, and Create Token. Vault and Pool follow in private beta, with more surfaces on the roadmap.

Unified Balance aggregates USDC across six chains through Circle Gateway. Trade pairs USDC ↔ EURC on the Arc-native Uniswap V2 deployment, with CCTP V2 burn-and-mint bridging in the same surface. Create Token deploys an ERC-20 from a single wallet signature. More surfaces are in the pipeline and will roll out as they're ready.

Unified Balance — powered by Circle Gateway

This is the integration we spent the most time on. Spend USDC on Arc using funds that live on a different chain, with no manual bridge step. Gateway aggregates your USDC across Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Avalanche, Optimism, and Polygon, and presents one balance, one approval, one transaction.

The practical effect: your USDC is no longer fragmented across chains. Whatever sits in your wallet, wherever it sits, is spendable on Arc.

Trade — native swap + CCTP V2

USDC ↔ EURC instantly via the Arc-native Uniswap V2 deployment. No wrapped tokens, no synthetic stables. Need to pull USDC in from another chain? CCTP V2 burn-and-mint is in the same surface — Circle's official native USDC bridge, no third-party messaging layer in the path.

Create Token — one click, no toolchain

Deploy an ERC-20 on Arc by filling out a form. Pick a name, ticker, supply, decimals — sign once, mint on confirmation. No Hardhat, no Foundry, no Solidity compiler. Pay gas in USDC. Get a contract address you can verify on the Arc explorer.

Why now, why testnet

Arc Testnet has been usable for a while. The reason we waited until today: we wanted the Gateway integration solid before calling this launched. A trade venue without a credible "spend from anywhere" story is just another swap UI, and the space already has plenty of those.

What we needed:

  • EIP-6963 wallet detection that works the same way across MetaMask, Rabby, Coinbase Wallet, and the rest — no more "which provider is the page actually using?"
  • RDNS-based wallet priority so the wallet you actually picked is the one we use
  • Gateway flow polish — chain logos in the source picker, source filtering by balance, multi-source spend in a single signature
  • Honest empty states — when a chain has 0 balance, we hide it instead of showing a confusing zero

None of that work is glamorous, but it is the difference between a demo and something a real user can rely on.

How to try it

  1. Get testnet USDC. Open faucet.circle.com, pick any supported testnet (Sepolia, Base Sepolia, Arbitrum Sepolia, Fuji, OP Sepolia, Polygon Amoy), paste your wallet address. You'll get 10 USDC instantly.
  2. Open arcswap.net. Click Connect Wallet. Pick your wallet from the picker (we detect via EIP-6963 — no more guessing).
  3. Go to /balance. You'll see your USDC across every supported chain in one view. Pick a source, hit Spend, and the funds settle on Arc — no separate bridge tab.
  4. Trade or ship a token. Swap USDC ↔ EURC at /trade, or deploy your first ERC-20 at /token. Both run on Arc with sub-second finality.
Testnet only — no real money
Everything on ArcSwap right now is Arc Testnet. Tokens are not real, balances are not real, yields would not be real. Mainnet ETA is 2027 and gated on Arc's own L1 launch. If anyone offers you "early access" to ArcSwap mainnet or an ASWP token — that's a scam. We don't have a token.

The shape of the next few weeks

We're focused on three things:

  1. More source chains in Gateway. The list will grow as Circle extends Gateway support — we're plugging chains in the moment they're available.
  2. Bug bounty + audit prep. Even though we don't run our own contracts, the frontend itself matters. CSP, SRI pinning, IPFS contenthash — all the boring defense-in-depth work.
  3. Listening. Tell us what's confusing. We read every Discord message. The roadmap bends to whatever's painful in real use.
